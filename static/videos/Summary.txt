This multimedia attachment presents real-world experimental validation of the proposed control framework using the da Vinci Research Kit (dVRK). 
The videos demonstrate robust needle picking and gauze retrieval tasks where Control Barrier Functions (CBF) ensure collision avoidance against spherical and cylindrical obstacles. 
Additionally, a multi-stage suturing sequence highlights the integration of Reinforcement Learning with Control Lyapunov Functions (CLF) for precise needle insertion. 
Finally, a mock lung tumor resection experiment illustrates the controller's ability to maintain safety boundaries around sensitive vascular structures while executing cutting trajectories. 
These experiments collectively validate the efficacy of the combined RL, CLF, and CBF approach in automating complex surgical subtasks.
